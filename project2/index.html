Letters Space Distribution by Tamar Rucham
Letters space distribution is a project that aims to explore the distribution in space of glyphs in different text segments of varying languages. This landing page contains a small multiple of the different space distributions. Darker areas are more frequently visited per character in the given text. Each square is linked to a page dedicated to that text blob. On a specific text page you will find on the left a larger version of the space distribution of that text. On the right you will find a bubble chart representing the number of times a specific character appears in text. The original text is displayed on the bottom. Hovering over a bubble to reveal the underlying data in a tooltip - showing how many times the character actually appears. The two graphs are also linked - hovering on a pixel in the space distribution section highlights the relevant characters in the bubbles.
